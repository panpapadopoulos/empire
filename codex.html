Preamble

In the name of the Empire, of the Sovereign Kingdom of the Two Crowns, and of the Eternal Seat of Order,

We, the Supreme Thrones and the united peoples under their dominion, do hereby enact this Constitution as the supreme and binding law of the land.

Founded upon the sacred legacy of the Crowned Bird and the wisdom of the Ancients, this document establishes the divine doctrine of governance, the harmony of order and freedom, and the inviolable duties of ruler and subject.

Let this Constitution be the unbreakable covenant between throne and citizen, forged in justice, preserved in honor, and transmitted in the name of peace, sovereignty, and imperial truth — now and for all generations to come.

History of the Empire

Once upon a time…

There were two villages: VillageA, founded by Lishhhhmm, and VillageB, led by panosusa. The two villages were located very close to each other, separated only by a short sea passage.

As they developed, the two leaders decided to unite their efforts and establish a joint industry, aiming to boost their production. The industry was built in VillageB due to its vast available space, in contrast to VillageA, which was located on a small island and lacked the necessary geographical capacity.

This decision led to rapid development and significant economic power. For better management, a unified administrative center was established in VillageB, and the residents of VillageA gradually relocated there. To create the needed space, part of VillageB had to be demolished. The two villages were now united into what became known as The City, governed jointly by the two leaders.

Exploration

The two leaders frequently sent expeditions to explore the surrounding regions. On one such mission, a relatively underdeveloped village was discovered. After discussions between the expedition members and the local leadership, there was a clear interest in the industrial and administrative system of the City.

Formal negotiations quickly began, with the goal of integrating this third village into the industrial collaboration. Soon, the City’s two leaders, Lishhhhmm and panosusa, formed a proposal that built upon industrial cooperation and expanded it into an administrative union — leading to the foundation of an Empire.

The Proposal

  • Full access to the goods produced by the common industry, in exchange for a commitment to participate in the production process.
  • The City would provide the necessary infrastructure and expertise for the production tasks assigned to the new village.
  • Due to the greater investment made by the City, a core requirement of the agreement was the administrative unification of the villages and the proclamation of an Empire.
  • The two leaders of the City would be recognized as the sole and equal Emperors of the Empire.

Requirements for Entry

Any Village / Region / City / Duchy wishing to join must:

  1. Appoint a Duke as the local leader of the area.
  2. Possess, at a minimum, the following infrastructure:
    • 5 houses
    • Central administrative building
    • Central goods repository
    • Central station
  3. Operate, maintain, and develop the assigned production infrastructure, ensuring the minimum required production levels are met.

The leader of the third village accepted the proposal during an official event held in the City, and thus, the Empire was born. Immediately, work began on drafting a constitution, which was signed by the newly crowned Emperors.

A Golden Age had begun for all.

Constitution of the Empire

The full Articles are included below. Use the sidebar to jump to any Article.

Article 1

Article 1: The Emperors

The two equal Emperors, Lishhhhmm and panosusa, are the sole Heads of State and leaders of the Executive Authority. They exercise power for life and stand above all subjects of the Empire.

Powers:

  • Sign and ratify the Constitution.
  • Propose amendments to the Constitution, which are approved by a 2/3 majority of the Imperial Council.
  • Sign and ratify laws. They hold the right to refuse signature and return a law to the Council for revision up to three (3) times per law.
  • Propose new laws, approved by a simple majority (½ + 1).
  • Issue Imperial Decrees, which may be annulled only by a 2/3 majority of the Council.
  • Suspend Council sessions at will, unless a majority of the Council demands an emergency continuation due to urgent matters.
  • Serve as the exclusive administrators of the Imperial Capital.
  • Bear responsibility for common industry, industrial infrastructure development, and transportation of materials and people.
  • Exercise exclusive administrative authority over the shared territories and dimensions of the Empire.
  • Approve the incorporation of new regions into the Empire.
  • Impose penalties for violations of laws or of the Constitution.
Article 2

Article 2: Imperial Council

The Council consists of the two Emperors (who preside alternately and hold double votes) and the Dukes of the Imperial regions.

Powers:

  • Acts as the Legislative and Judicial Authority of the Empire.
  • May be convened by any member, with the presiding Emperor setting the session date.
  • Proposes and approves legislation by simple majority (½ + 1).
  • Proposes and approves constitutional amendments by enhanced majority (2/3).
  • Provides counsel to the Emperors.
  • May annul an Imperial Decree by a 2/3 majority.
  • Functions as the Supreme Court, resolving disputes between regions.
  • Serves as the Imperial Court. Members bear the title “Governmental Lords” when present in the Capital and must wear official attire and the crown bestowed upon them when in the Capital or in the presence of an Emperor.
Article 3

Article 3: Regions of the Empire

Each new Region (Village / City / Duchy) joining the Empire must meet the following requirements:

Required Infrastructure:

  • Central Administrative Building with an Imperial Throne for the Duke (only if no Emperor is present).
  • Central Goods Repository.
  • At least five (5) residences.
  • A Production Unit managed according to required quotas and supplying designated materials to the Imperial Center.
  • A Central Station connected to the Capital through the Imperial Network.

General Principles:

  • All infrastructure must be designed in coordination with the Emperors, following architectural styles compatible with the region’s biome.
  • All public buildings must display at least one Imperial flag, flown higher than any other symbol.

Rights of the Duke:

  • May appoint assistant administrators.
  • May request titles of nobility for assistants (Count, Viscount, Baron) from the Emperors, subject solely to the Emperors’ discretion.

Imperial Offices:

The Empire may operate one or more Imperial Offices in any Region to inform citizens about:

  • The history and values of the Empire,
  • Infrastructure and projects overseen by the Emperors,
  • Applicable laws, decrees, and regulations.

Installation and operation require Imperial approval and oversight.

Article 4

Article 4: Admission of New Regions

Admission of a new Region follows this process:

  1. Submission of Application
    The Community submits a formal application to the Imperial Council.
  2. Council Approval
    The Council deliberates and decides by simple majority (½ + 1). The decision is forwarded to the Emperors.
  3. Imperial Veto Power
    The Emperors retain absolute veto authority.
    • If both exercise veto, admission is permanently rejected.
    • If only one exercises veto, the matter returns to the Council for review.
    • If the Council reapproves by a 2/3 majority, the decision becomes binding.
  4. Final Ratification
    Upon acceptance, the Emperors ratify admission through a special Decree of Accession.
Article 5

Article 5: Titles and Noble Hierarchy

The Emperors exclusively grant noble titles as rewards for service, loyalty, or contribution to the Empire.

Titles:

  • Count
  • Viscount
  • Marquess
  • Baron
  • Knight

The Duke ranks above them, and the Emperors are above all of them.

Grant and Recognition:

  • Titles are conferred through official ceremony and royal decree.
  • Nobles may wear official attire and insignia.
  • Titles may include privileges such as:
    • Access to central buildings or ceremonies,
    • Decorative coats of arms on estates,
    • Ceremonial positions at Capital events,
    • Advisory (non-voting) roles in the Council.

Revocation:

  • Any title may be revoked by the Emperors with written justification for dishonorable conduct or treason.
Article 6

Article 6: Rights and Duties of Citizens

Citizens are recognized as free subjects with fundamental rights and obligations.

Rights:

  1. Right to Property.
  2. Right to Protection from arbitrary actions or punishment.
  3. Right to Petition and Hearing.
  4. Freedom of Movement within the Empire.

Obligations:

  1. Respect for Laws.
  2. Obedience to Authorities.
  3. Civic Participation in public works and regional defense.
Article 7

Article 7: The Imperial Capital

The Capital is the supreme administrative, spiritual, ceremonial, and productive center of the Empire, formed from the two original regions of the Emperors, now known as “The City”.

I. Governance

  • The Capital is governed exclusively by the Emperors or by representatives formally appointed by them.
  • No other official holds governing authority within the Capital unless explicitly appointed by Imperial Decree.

II. Core Infrastructure

The Capital will include the following central infrastructures:

  1. Imperial Palace
    The residence and administrative manor of the Emperors; the seat of Decrees and of the high councils.
  2. Council Hall
    The official chamber of the Imperial Council, where matters of supreme political importance are debated and decided.
  3. Plaza of Ceremonies
    A public space designated for ceremonies, official proclamations, honors, and executions. It is the site of imperial coronations, the granting of noble titles, and the reception of foreign delegations.
  4. Imperial Temple of the Arts
    A sacred structure where rituals of restoration, enhancement, and magical transformation of objects of power and honor are performed. Access to its inner sanctums is restricted exclusively to the priests of the Order of the Enchanters and the Emperors.
  5. Central Imperial Storehouse
    The primary repository for raw materials, food supplies, weapons, and valuable goods. It is overseen by a Chief Storekeeper appointed by the Emperors.
  6. Central Industrial District
    A regulated industrial complex where the production of rare materials and strategic imperial resources is concentrated. It serves as the industrial backbone of the Empire.
  7. Central Capital Station
    The main transportation hub connecting the Capital to all Provinces through the Imperial Transportation Network.
  8. Imperial Museum and Library
    The institution entrusted with preserving the history of the Empire, the Constitution, Imperial Decrees, and its most significant artifacts.

III. High Commissariats of the Regions

  • Every region that is part of the Empire has the right to maintain a High Commissariat within the Capital.
  • The High Commissioner serves as the official representative of the region in the Capital.
  • Each Commissariat functions to:
    • Coordinate with the Imperial Administration,
    • Monitor decisions made at the central level,
    • Present petitions and requests from the region to the Council or the Emperors.
  • High Commissioners may also be the Dukes themselves and are entitled to maintain a personal residence and office within the Capital.

IV. Conduct and Sanctity

  • The Capital is considered sacred ground. Any act of destruction, disorder, or irreverence constitutes treason and is punishable by the severest penalties.
  • Citizens and officials are required to wear official attire when entering public buildings.
  • The Imperial Flag must fly continuously on all buildings of the Capital.
Article 8

Article 8: Imperial Symbols — Banner and Emblem

  1. The official Imperial Banner consists of a white field bearing a green bird in the upper corner, symbolizing the spirit of exploration that led to the founding of the Empire.
  2. The lower half of the Banner displays a green diagonal triangle, representing fertile lands and the industrious labor that sustain the Empire’s growth and prosperity.
  3. At the upper center of the Banner appears the green Bird itself — an ancient symbol of wisdom, vigilance, and divine mandate entrusted to the Empire’s rulers. The Bird is depicted as eternally flying above the lands and peoples of the Empire, guardian of unity and freedom.
  4. The Imperial Emblem reproduces the Banner’s design, enhanced by two golden crowns placed upon the head of the Crowned Bird, symbolizing the equal and sovereign authority of the Dual Emperors, Lishhhhmm and panosusa.
  5. Both the Banner and the Emblem must be displayed at all Imperial ceremonies, official buildings, and military standards, serving as constant reminders of the bond between ruler and subject and of the enduring power of the Empire under the Twin Crowns.
Article 9

Article 9: The Order of the Enchanters

The Order of the Enchanters is the Empire’s sacred and technical order, entrusted with the guardianship of magical technologies and sacred artifacts of power. Its existence is inseparably bound to the will of the Emperors, from whom it derives its authority, structure, and purpose.

Mission

  • To conduct rituals that strengthen, recharge, and protect magical artifacts.
  • To ensure the correct and responsible use of powerful artifacts throughout the Empire.
  • To train new Enchanters and preserve the Archives of the Ancient Arts.

Governance

  • The Order answers directly and exclusively to the Emperors, who are its sole and highest authority.
  • No internal rank or office exists independently of the Emperors. All titles and roles within the Order are granted, supervised, and revoked solely by Imperial command.
  • The Enchanters are accountable to no other authority, administrative or religious.

Privileges and Duties

  • Members bear distinctive insignia (such as a Crystal Scepter or Bracelet of Flame).
  • They possess unrestricted access to magical infrastructure and may attend rituals of the highest power.
  • They hold no legislative authority, serving instead as technical and spiritual advisors to the Emperors on matters of magic, technology, and power.
Article 10

Article 10: Final Provision

This document constitutes the supreme legal authority of the Empire and the source from which all power derives.

By the will of the Emperors and the united peoples of the Empire, this Constitution stands as the eternal foundation of our Sovereign Realm. May it guide us with wisdom, uphold justice through strength, and unite us in peace and prosperity for all ages.

Issued in the Capital of the Empire, under the vigilance of the Twin Crowns and the sacred legacy of the Crowned Bird, this day marks the dawn of a new era — steadfast, sovereign, and eternal.
